The Junior Affiliate Program

Our Junior Membership program is designed to engage the next generation in Mayflower heritage and history.

Program Benefits

  • We encourage our members to enroll their children and grandchildren as Juniors
  • Our Junior enrollment fee is only $10.00
  • We believe that families become more engaged in the Mayflower heritage when children are included in this way
  • Our Juniors Committee tries to keep in contact with these youngsters and our newsletter sometimes includes a special section for them
  • Between the ages of 18 to 21, our Juniors are "retired" as Junior enrollees and encouraged to join as regular members

Educational Programs

The Capital Colony Library Program

The Capital Colony maintains a library of Mayflower-related materials and genealogical resources available to members for research purposes.

Educational Presentations

The society offers educational programs throughout the year, including:

  • Historical presentations about the Mayflower voyage and Plymouth Colony
  • Genealogical research workshops
  • Guest speakers on Colonial American history
  • Youth education programs

Colony Activities

The Alabama Society is organized into four regional colonies:

  • Capital Colony - Montgomery area
  • Gulf Coast Colony - Mobile area
  • Tennessee Valley Colony - Huntsville area
  • Cahaba River Colony - Birmingham area

Each colony hosts local meetings and events throughout the year. Contact your colony representative for more information about upcoming activities.

Annual Events

The Alabama Society hosts several annual events including:

  • Spring Conference and Luncheon
  • Compact Day Observance (November)
  • Educational seminars and workshops
  • Colony meetings and gatherings

For current event information, please contact us or visit our new website at alabamasmd.org.
